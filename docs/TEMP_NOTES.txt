non architecture problem:
- updating transfer doesn't show where it comes from and where it is targeting, the to part should be open for editing 
  - can be fixed from the frontend side
- transfers and transactions have shared container (can be fixed from frontend side), this creates clarity problems for
  later
- TransferCard s doesn't show which to and from they are

- old architecture:
  - uses transaction type to distinguish between adding and withdrawing money from the system
  - income is wrapped in budget `is_income` property and treated as a budget
  => this architecture mixes between income management and free budget management, creating ambiguous

- our app goal:
  - income and spending is clear and locked, with honest view of financial condition
  - has integration with bank APIs
  - liability management

- why old architecture clashes w/ our goal:
  - mixing between income spending management / freestyle budget management => worst of both worlds
    -> loose view of financial modules
  - hard for integration with banking APIs, as they don't anchor a fixed place for importing bank statements
  - for later, money from multiple sources will break the terms & record 

- why was old architecture implemented?
  - simplicity
  - easy learning curve

=> this pathway offers simplicity but add more problems for future use and development

- new architecture proposed:
  - incomes stand as an independent row that logs incomes only
  - transactions logs spending only
  - budgets only counts for spending budgets 
  - allocations helps with moving income to budgets
  - transfers helps with moving money from budget to budget
  - (for later): liability helps with loans & owes

- why this works: 
  - clear and clean purpose of data models
  - easy for later integration

- difficulties:
  - midway - consumes time and work
  - rewrite a lot of stuff
